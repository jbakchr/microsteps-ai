### Input:

- Step 1: Take 5 deep breaths in through nose out through mouth.

- Step 2: Notice the sensation of feet on ground or floor.
- Step 3: Stretch arms up high, then release with a sigh.

### Felt (quick impression):

- 👍 Good (and useful)

#### Short note

Although a first and very easy step could have just been like "lie down on your couch", and perhaps the next two being "close laptop" (or put away electronic devices) and then "take 5 deep breaths", this first step of "Take 5 deep breaths in through nose out through mouth." actually made me do at least this one right away which was (kinda surprisingly) very useful start my kinda needed process in the moment to get at least more relaxed. And from just that I didn't feel relaxed BUT I did feel like "Okay, this is good, I need to relax some more right now" after just doing this one simple first step. So, that was great. Because from just - in my case doing - one simple thing led me to do some more related things myself in order to relax (whereto the suggested two next steps didn't feel like something I HAD to do - the whole things made me feel like "Okay, Jonas, do one breathing thing to relax and then just .. relax some more in anyway you like" ).

### Action:

- ✅ Did step 1 (which felt like enough in the moment)
- ❌ Did not step 2 and 3 as they felt too "far fetched" and sort of too - i don't know - "new age" like "feel the grass between your toes" kinda (too "hippie" for me)

### Optional note: why / why not

As mentioned above: just by taking step 1 and breathing to relax I sort of didn't need - although it was good to have more suggestions - more steps to get myself to relax. Just one suggestion for trying to relax was in some way enough to get me to do a bit more relaxing things by myself.
